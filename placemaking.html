PLACEMAKING DESIGN INTERVENTION

Semester 9 Design Project (2019)


Jump to







THE AIM

1. Data collection and representation- To create a palette of alternative ways to look at collecting data and representation which act as tools for driving a design program and intervention.
2. Lens and non-linear Program/Design Generation- Understanding of some critical lenses through which Architectural sites can be decoded, narrowed down to, & looked upon with precision â€“ paving the way for strategic program development and subsequent tangible design interventions.
3. Design Investigation- To study the architectural potential of the social spaces of the street, which are in need of an updated design investigation.



THE TEAM

Sanjana Danait, Vaidehi Kane



MY ROLE

All work involving site study, initial research, interviews, design ideation, design drawings, panel compositions, 3D modelling and rendering, was equally distributed and conducted by both team members.

THE SITE

Location- Kika Street, Bhuleshwar, Mumbai, India
The site given was the stretch of Kika street and C.P.Tank road, along with Gulalwadi Circle, the busy junction where these two streets meet. The two streets have mostly old buildings, and are famous for their steel and hardware markets. Almost all buildings on this stretch are mix use buildings, commercial on the ground floor and residential on the rest of the floors. Gulalwadi Circle is a major Labour Hub where several daily wage laborers sit all day, seeking employment.



The Site: Kika Street, Bhuleshwar, Mumbai, India



DESIGN PROCESS

The initial process involved studying the site based on research deliverables and a lens. Each group had one lens, and two research deliverables. The site study was done according to Research Deliverables a) Programmatic Analysis and b) Figure Ground Study. The first RD (Programmatic Analysis) studied the various activities at different times during the day through observation and interviews. The second RD studied the density on site, both of buildings and of the resultant activities and people.
The data thus collected was then analysed through the lens- Binary Conflicts. This lens focused on identifying engaging conflicts at multiple levels using analytical tools with a base in social, political, economical, materiality, etc. The intent was to use this contrasting/conflicting energy as a stimulus for intervention or a critical lens through which the site can be analysed and an architectural idea generated.

Panel 1

Panel 1 depicts one of the most striking binaries on this site - Day/Night. Since the area is primarily residential and commercial, there is continuous influx of goods,and circulation of vendors, labourers, pedestrians, customers, and residents on the street. In contrast, the street becomes almost deserted at night as the commercial activity stops, the laborers move out and residents sleep. The Gulalwadi Circle, a buzzing labour hub and taxi stand during the day, becomes a khau galli during the night.

Panel 2

The Second step of the process was to explore possible activities and functions that could be integrated in the street based on the programmatic analysis of the site. This panel depicts the exploration of interventions like- Parks to provide breathing spaces in the densely populated site; Shaded resting space for labourers; An ephemeral open theatre on the street with the hathgadis doubling up as street furniture to encourage social interaction; Skill development workshops for women empowerment.

Placemaking: Panel 2 (Click on image to enlarge & zoom)


Panel 3

On observing the the noticeable lack of opinions in the women of Kika street, and their complete financial dependence on their husbands, we conducted extensive interviews with them. Most women mentioned that if given the opportunity, they wold like to learn and teach the arts and skills they had given up in the past, to work towards financial independence. Interviews with the laborers at Gulalwadi indicated their need for a shelter with basic amenities. The senior and experienced labourers also showed willingness to teach their skills to youngsters in the trade, to make some extra money.
The site was changed to Kika Mansion, at the intersection of Kika street and Gulalwadi junction. The design proposal included-
a) Redevelopment of Kika mansion- residential & commercial, b) Skill development and women empowerment centre during the day/ and a night school for the laborers, driving the main binary of day and night.

Placemaking: Panel 3 (Click on image to enlarge & zoom)


Panel 4

This panel depicts the final formulated program intent based on interview statistics-
a) Skill development & women empowerment centre- an attempt to make women independent by leveraging their skills. The intervention also takes into consideration the lack of affordable day-care services available for these women and hence play spaces for children have been integrated within the proposal.
b) Night school for labourers- An opportunity for labourers to learn and take formal education and to teach their own skills to others, making some extra money in the process.
c) Redevelopment of Kika mansion- housing & shops
d) The street- An interactive thoroughfare manifested such that it will be acts as a congregation space/ ephemeral open theatre/ khau galli during the night to allow the culture of Gulalwadi to seep into the intervention

Placemaking: Panel 4 (Click on image to enlarge & zoom)


FINAL DESIGN

The women's centre and night school design has a courtyard, and open corridors for visual interaction and communication. The ground floor has shops, with arched gateways bringing in some elements of the Kika mansion. The redeveloped housing building is a high rise, but still brings in the essence of their current chawl system - communication and interaction.
