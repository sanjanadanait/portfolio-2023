SMART TRAFFIC MANAGEMENT

48hr Challenge- Research & Lo-fidelity Design (2021)


Jump to







OVERVIEW

Traffic Management is one of the biggest problems faced by most developing countries today. Cities are getting over populated, the use of private cars is increasing, and building additional new infrastructure in congested cities is an expensive and herculean task. Traffic woes are also affecting the functioning of emergency vehicles like ambulances, fire brigades, which ultimately results in loss of lives.
In such a scenario, newer, smarter and cost effective solutions have to be implemented. Smart traffic monitoring and management is one such way to deal with the ever rising traffic issues in cities. This system will not only reduce traffic woes but will eventually also reduce and avoid accidents on roads, lead to less air pollution, and less wastage of fuel.

RESEARCH

The steps to come up with an efficient solution for traffic management include:

For the data collection, identifying what scenarios should be monitored is the first step


Scenarios to be monitored:

  • Peak traffic timings and routes with usual bottlenecks
  • Accident information (accident prone zones, number of accidents per day, fatalities, assistance time)
  • Rule violations (Speeding/ Red light violations)
  • Parking information (Illegal parking spots, double parking on streets, empty parking spots)
  • Traffic signal timings
  • Traffic police distribution

System Requirements to monitor the scenarios:

  • Speed sensors
  • Smart traffic lights
  • Queue Detectors
  • Red light violation detection cameras
  • Automatic number plate recognition cameras
  • V2V communication software
  • Variable message sign boards
  • Smart parking sensors

Assumptions:

  • Availability of smartphones and internet connection with each and every person in the city
  • Centralized transport department that handles all public transport as well as roadways
  • Centralized city parking system

Constraints/ Challenges:

  • Current cities already have infrastructure issues like road planning, zoning, etc. that could pose problems when implementing the traffic management system
  • Hi tech systems like V2V communication will be present only in newer car models
  • All hi tech solutions will need high speed internet connectivity. If at any given time the internet connectivity is disrupted, it will disrupt the entire system as it functions on real time data
  • Cyber security and data privacy becomes a big challenge as a large number of devices will be accessing a central network

Trade-offs:

  • Cost
  • Time required to implement and install all system requirements

USER TYPES & WIREFRAMES

In this Smart Traffic Management System design, there will be 4 types of end users and their goals will vary accordingly. Thus, the system will have to be designed with 4 different user interfaces based on the changing use cases and goals.


User type 1: The Super Admin

This user will be the person working in the main traffic control room of the city who will have the most important role to play in this system.
Goals:

  • Get access to all the data collected by the smart devices within the system
  • Analyse the collected data
  • Manage traffic, routes, diversions
  • Manage users
  • Manage traffic police
  • View violations, E-challans, accidents data
  • Communicate with Sub Admins/ Traffic Polic

User type 2: The Sub Admin

These users will be the ones working in the traffic sub control rooms of the city. For example, in a city like Mumbai, apart from the main control room, each ward can have their own traffic sub control room. This way, each ward can manage their own traffic police, attend to emergency calls, and diversions if necessary.
Goals:

  • Manage traffic & diversions
  • Manage and communicate with traffic police
  • View accidents data, attend to emergency calls

PART 2 BRIEF - TRAFFIC MANAGEMENT APP FOR TRAFFIC POLICE

To understand the problems specifically faced by traffic police currently and design a user friendly interface for a traffic management app.



PROBLEM STATEMENT

Traffic police have to work in extreme weather conditions and long durations to keep the city traffic in control. Controlling congestion and traffic jams during peak hours, festivals, VIP visits, rains as well as managing emergency accident cases is a time-consuming and difficult task especially in harsh weather conditions. Sometimes, catching violators on roads in peak traffic causes more traffic jams and inconvenience to the traffic police as well as the general public. There is a need to come up with easy and quick solutions to make the lives of traffic police easier.



PROJECT OBJECTIVES

  • To reduce the manpower required at each major signal/junction which will lead to reduced work hours of traffic police constables as well as better manpower distribution to all major junctions of the city
  • To reduce number of devices used by the traffic police for monitoring and managing traffic
  • Design a system that is easy to use and that reduces time and efforts needed by the user currently to perform a certain task
  • To make it easier for the traffic police to clock-in & clock-out without having to spend time and energy on commuting to the police station
  • To make it easier for the traffic police to manage emergency scenarios like accidents, fire, VIP visits, etc.

USER STUDY

Goals of the user interviews

  • To understand the daily schedule of a traffic police constable
  • To understand how the current traffic monitoring and management system works
  • To understand duties of the traffic police
  • To find out about their pain points
  • To find out what features of the current system they like
  • To understand future traffic monitoring ideas that the government intends to implement
  • How do they interact with nearby traffic police?
  • How do they interact with the traffic control room?
  • Is technology helping in making their jobs easier or is it becoming more difficult?

Interview Insights

  • Currently the traffic police in Mumbai are extremely overworked. Their shift timings can vary from 8-10hrs a day and can go up to 14-16hrs a day including the time they spend in commuting.
  • Traffic police are required to go to police stations to clock-in before reporting to their assigned duty location. This is very time consuming for them and makes their already long hours even more hectic.
  • In Mumbai, smart traffic cameras to detect red light violations at major signals and speed violations at highways have already been implemented. So the traffic police donâ€™t need to catch these violators on the ground.
  • Their duties include catching violations like drivers not wearing helmets, seat belt violations, driving on the wrong side, etc. Duties vary according to situations as well, for example, catching violations like not wearing a mask have been added to their duties post COVID-19.
  • Currently the traffic police system operates in a way where 3-4 constables are assigned at major signals and junctions for catching violators and maneuvering traffic jams. Out of the 4 constables, 2 of them handle violations and generate E-challans through the handheld challan device. The other 2 constables navigate the traffic.
  • Managing too many devices- their mobile phones, walkie talkies, as well as the e challan device is a challenging task.
  • Not all traffic police constables are tech savvy and find it difficult to keep up with complex technology.
